Neural Networks: The Brains of Modern AI

Neural networks are at the core of many modern AI systems. They try to mimic how the human brain works by using interconnected layers of nodes, or neurons.

Neural networks are the backbone of deep learning.

  • Geoffrey Hinton

A neural network learns by adjusting weights and biases to minimize error.

How They Work

A simple neural network consists of an input layer, one or more hidden layers, and an output layer.

  • Input Layer
  • Hidden Layers
  • Output Layer
  1. Data enters the network.
  2. It passes through hidden layers where computations happen.
  3. The network makes a prediction.

Neural networks are trained using an algorithm called backpropagation. Here’s a tiny example in Python:

import tensorflow as tf

model = tf.keras.Sequential([
    tf.keras.layers.Dense(10, activation='relu'),
    tf.keras.layers.Dense(1)
])

They use calculus to update weights. For example, the gradient descent uses:

wnew=wold−η∂E∂w w_{new} = w_{old} - \eta \frac{\partial E}{\partial w} wnew​=wold​−η∂w∂E​

You can see that in this equation, the weight update depends on the learning rate η\etaη and the derivative of the error EEE.

Why They Matter

Neural networks power many applications:

  • Image recognition
  • Natural language processing
  • Autonomous driving
