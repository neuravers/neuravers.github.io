About me

My adventure with computer science began with a playful twist — I’ve always loved making people laugh, and that fun spirit led me to discover programming. Back in primary school, I got hooked on Minecraft, fascinated by the idea of creating my own mods. That curiosity pushed me to learn Java, but I struggled to set up my coding environment because the book I had was too technical for a beginner, so I set Java aside for years… until recently. For me, programming is a gift: it lets me build anything from scratch with no need for physical materials — my imagination is the only limit. Over time, I’ve worked on fun projects, like a harmless troll virus my friends and I snuck onto a teacher’s computer, popping up silly messages that had us in stitches. Later, I created my first game in Unreal Engine and dove into AI and mathematics to push my skills further. Each project taught me I can learn anything if I keep it fun and intresting. Right now, I’m focused on AI because its potential aligns perfectly with my values.
